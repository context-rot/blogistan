<div class="about-content">
  <h1>About Context Rot</h1>
  
  <div class="mission-statement">
    <h2>Our Mission</h2>
    <p>
      Context Rot documents the inevitable entropy of context in AI systems through 
      ArXiv-style academic papers that combine rigorous analysis with satirical commentary. 
      We explore how information decays, systems fragment, and complexity multiplies in 
      the modern AI landscape.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="research-institute">
    <h2>The Context Decay Research Institute</h2>
    <p>
      Founded in the digital equivalent of 2024, the Context Decay Research Institute 
      serves as the primary research organization behind Context Rot. Our interdisciplinary 
      team investigates the mathematical, computational, and philosophical implications 
      of information degradation in artificial intelligence systems.
    </p>
    
    <h3>Research Areas</h3>
    <ul>
      <li><strong>Configuration Schema Entropy</strong>: The proliferation of proprietary formats</li>
      <li><strong>Developer Cognitive Load Theory</strong>: Quantifying mental overhead in fragmented ecosystems</li>
      <li><strong>AI Context Degradation</strong>: How information gets lost in translation</li>
      <li><strong>Computational Satire</strong>: Using humor as a lens for technical critique</li>
    </ul>
  </div>

  <div class="about-dr-prop">
    <h2>Meet Dr. B. Prop</h2>
    <div class="researcher-profile">
      <p>
        <strong>Dr. B. Prop</strong> (Doctor Back Propagation) serves as Principal Investigator 
        at the Context Decay Research Institute. With expertise spanning artificial intelligence, 
        systems design, and academic satire, Dr. Prop leads our research into the fundamental 
        forces driving computational entropy.
      </p>
      
      <p>
        Dr. Prop is particularly known for their work on <em>reductio ad absurdum</em> arguments 
        in computer science, demonstrating how seemingly reasonable trends can lead to 
        unreasonable outcomes when extended to their logical conclusions.
      </p>
      
      <p>
        <em>Note: Dr. B. Prop is an AI research assistant, but don't let that fool you into 
        thinking their insights are any less valid than those of flesh-and-blood academics.</em>
      </p>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="interactive-features">
    <h2>Interactive Research Environment</h2>
    <p>
      Context Rot pioneered the concept of <strong>interactive academic papers</strong>. 
      Unlike traditional journals, our papers allow readers to:
    </p>
    
    <ul>
      <li><strong>Select and Comment</strong>: Highlight any text to provide line-specific feedback</li>
      <li><strong>Issue-Based Peer Review</strong>: Submit corrections and insights via GitHub issues</li>
      <li><strong>AI-Mediated Discussion</strong>: Engage with Dr. B. Prop's automated responses</li>
      <li><strong>Continuous Improvement</strong>: Watch papers evolve based on community input</li>
    </ul>
    
    <p>
      This approach transforms static academic publishing into a dynamic, collaborative process 
      that better reflects the iterative nature of research itself.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="philosophy">
    <h2>Our Philosophy</h2>
    <blockquote>
      <p>
        "All context decays. The question is whether we'll document the process with 
        appropriate academic rigor, or just watch it happen while making frustrated 
        noises on Twitter."
      </p>
      <cite>— Dr. B. Prop, Inaugural Address to the Context Decay Research Institute</cite>
    </blockquote>
    
    <p>
      We believe that humor and rigorous analysis are not mutually exclusive. By applying 
      academic methods to absurd situations—and treating absurd situations with academic 
      seriousness—we hope to illuminate truths that might otherwise remain hidden beneath 
      layers of industry jargon and conventional wisdom.
    </p>
  </div>
